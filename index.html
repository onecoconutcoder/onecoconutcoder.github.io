ArtSpeech


Leveraging Articulatory Representations for Adaptive Text-to-Speech Synthesis

Paper Introduction

Abstract. We propose ArtSpeech, a human-like text-to-speech (TTS) model, by revisiting the articulatory system. Current deep TTS models learn the acoustic-text mapping in a fully parametric manner, ignoring the latent articulation movement and lacking explainability. ArtSpeech, on the contrary, leverages articulatory representations to perform adaptive TTS, clearly describing the voice tone and speaking prosody of different speakers. Specifically, energy, pitch, and vocal tract variables (TVs) are utilized to represent airflow forced by articulatory organs, the degree of tension in the vocal folds of the larynx, and the coordinated movements between different organs, respectively. For TTS synthesis, we guide the generative process with style adaptors and variation predictors during training. Compared with parametric approaches, ArtSpeech performs better on zero-shot TTS synthesis and greatly boosts the similarity between target speeches and the generated results.

Style Combination and Interpolation

ArtSpeech employs a variety of independent articulatory style vectors to decouple the speaking styles of the target speaker. This enables the creation of unique speaking styles, distinct from the reference audio, by freely combining or interpolating these style vectors.
